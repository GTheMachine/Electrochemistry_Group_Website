Research Areas

Our laboratory focuses on innovative electrochemical research to solve pressing energy and environmental challenges. We take a multidisciplinary approach that combines materials science, chemistry, physics, and engineering principles.

Advanced Energy Storage

Our team investigates novel materials and architectures for next-generation batteries and supercapacitors. Current focus areas include:

  • High-capacity electrode materials
  • Solid-state electrolytes with enhanced ionic conductivity
  • Nanostructured interfaces for improved stability
  • Computational modeling of electrochemical processes

Sustainable Materials

Our research aims to develop environmentally friendly materials for electrochemical applications with reduced environmental impact:

  • Bio-derived carbon materials
  • Recycling and upcycling of battery components
  • Low-cost earth-abundant materials
  • Life cycle assessment of electrochemical technologies

Research Facilities

Our state-of-the-art laboratory is equipped with advanced instrumentation for materials synthesis, electrochemical characterization, and surface analysis:

Materials Synthesis

  • Hydrothermal/solvothermal reactors
  • Chemical vapor deposition system
  • Plasma-enhanced deposition
  • Sol-gel processing equipment

Electrochemical Analysis

  • Potentiostats/galvanostats
  • Impedance analyzers
  • Battery testing systems
  • In-situ/operando capabilities

Characterization

  • Scanning electron microscopy
  • X-ray diffraction
  • Raman spectroscopy
  • X-ray photoelectron spectroscopy
